# Prologue – The Moment Between

*Freely inspired by the idea of "The Egg" by Andy Weir*
*Retold in my own words, with gratitude*

---

You died.

Not dramatically. Not with fanfare.
One moment you were there – and then you weren't.

And now you are here.

Wherever *here* is.

---

There is light. Not the blinding kind. The warm kind. The kind that feels like the first morning of spring when you open a window and the air smells like rain and earth and something you cannot name but have always known.

You are standing – or floating, it is hard to tell – in a space that has no edges.

And there is someone with you.

Not a stranger. Not exactly.

"What happened?" you ask.

"You died," the presence says. Simply. Without sadness.

"And now?"

"Now we talk."

---

You have questions. Of course you do. Everyone does.

*Was it worth it? Did I do enough? Was I good?*

The presence listens to all of them.

"You were human," it says finally. "That means you were everything. Brave and afraid. Generous and selfish. Awake some days. Sleepwalking on others."

"That doesn't answer my question."

"No," it agrees. "It doesn't."

---

There is a pause. The kind of pause that has weight.

Then the presence says something that changes everything:

"You have always been here before."

You look up.

"Every person who has ever lived on that planet – every farmer, every child, every king, every beggar, every saint, every criminal – was you. In another time. In another body. Living another life."

You try to understand this.

"And every person who will ever live," it continues, "is also you. Still to come. Still learning."

"Why?" you ask.

The answer is quiet but absolute:

"Because you are not finished yet."

---

You stand in the light for a long time.

Or maybe no time at all. Time works differently here.

And then the presence asks:

**"Who do you want to be next?"**

---

There is no wrong answer.

There is no test.

There is only the question – and the infinite space of what is possible.

You can choose a life of community. Of Ubuntu. Of *I am because we are.*
You can choose a life of adventure. Of ocean and wind and freedom.
You can choose to walk the path that most people walk – work, consume, sleep, repeat – and see where it leads.
You can choose something no one has chosen yet.

The choice is yours.

It has always been yours.

---

*Below, the Earth turns slowly.*
*Billions of lives, unfolding simultaneously.*
*Each one a story.*
*Each one written by someone who was brave enough to choose.*

**Which path do you take?**

---

*Read the paths others have chosen.*
*Or write your own.*

*The book is open.*
*The pen is yours.*
